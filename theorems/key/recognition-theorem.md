# THE RECOGNITION THEOREM
## Window syzygies decouple sheet by sheet: the census is q-free

*A standalone component of **The Sofa Theorem** — a proof of Degtyarev–Shimada Conjecture 1.2 for the 3^v Fermat tower. Rafael Amichis Luengo (Madrid, tretoef@gmail.com) and Claude (Anthropic). Repository: github.com/tretoef-estrella/sofa-theorem.*

---

**Scope.** the theorem below is proven in full for every tower level and every coefficient degree `e < q`. It makes the window syzygy census a FIXED, q-independent, finite linear-algebra problem. It does not by itself close the main theorem; it is the solving tool for the census. 

---

### 0. Setting

`S = F₃[s₀..s₅]`, `E = (e₁,e₃,e₅) = ∩_J I_J` radical (Odd Symmetric, pillar i), the 15 sheets `L_J` (matchings of K₆), `R = S/E`, `q = 3^v`. For a **pair** `{a,b} ⊆ {0..5}` let `V({a,b}) := ∪_{J ∋ {a,b}} L_J` (the three sheets whose matching contains the edge) and `I({a,b}) := I(V({a,b}))`.

**Lemma 0 (the edge ideal).** `I({a,b}) = (s_a + s_b) + (ε₁^{(ab)}, ε₃^{(ab)})`, where `ε_k^{(ab)}` is the k-th elementary symmetric polynomial of the four variables other than `s_a, s_b`.
*Proof.* Every sheet through the edge satisfies `s_a+s_b = 0`, and its restriction to that hyperplane is a matching-sheet of the K₄ on the remaining four variables; the union of the three K₄-sheets has radical ideal `(ε₁^{(ab)}, ε₃^{(ab)})` — the K₄ instance of the Odd Symmetric Theorem (the intersection of the K₄ matching ideals is generated by the odd elementary symmetrics). Adding the hyperplane form gives the ideal of the union. ∎

### 1. The Recognition Theorem

> **Theorem R.** Let `q = 3^v` and let `λ = (λ₀,…,λ₅)` be homogeneous of degree `e < q`. Then
> `Σ_{i=0}^{5} λ_i·s_i^q ∈ E   ⟺   λ_a − λ_b ∈ I({a,b})` **for every one of the 15 edges** `{a,b}` of K₆.
> Moreover the right-hand condition does not involve `q`: **for `e < q`, the space `Syz_e` of window syzygies is canonically independent of the tower level.**

*Proof.* `F := Σλ_i s_i^q ∈ E ⟺ F` vanishes on every sheet (E radical). Fix a sheet `J` with pairs `(a_k, b_k)`; on `L_J`, `s_{b_k} = −s_{a_k}`, and with sheet coordinates `u_k := s_{a_k}|_{L_J}`:
`F|_{L_J} = Σ_{k=1}^{3} (λ_{a_k} − λ_{b_k})|_{L_J} · u_k^q`
(using `s_{b_k}^q = −u_k^q`, q odd). Each summand is `u_k^q · g_k` with `deg g_k = e < q`; two monomials `u_k^q·m` and `u_l^q·m′` with `k ≠ l` can never coincide (each would need exponent ≥ q in two different slots while the cofactors have degree < q). Hence the three summands have disjoint monomial supports, and `F|_{L_J} = 0 ⟺ (λ_{a_k} − λ_{b_k})|_{L_J} = 0` for each k. Collecting the condition over the three sheets through a fixed edge `{a,b}` gives exactly `λ_a − λ_b ∈ I({a,b})` (Lemma 0). The conditions are linear constraints on degree-e forms with no reference to q. ∎

**Remark (sharpness).** The decoupling uses only `e < q`; at `e = q` (degree `2q`, the window's top edge) monomial collisions `u_k^q u_l^q` become possible and the statement is exactly where the Zone-C boundary analysis (Theorem Z⁺ and the `d = 2q` residue) takes over. The theorem covers the entire open window `q ≤ d ≤ 2q−1`.

### 2. Corollaries

**Corollary 1 (q-free census).** For each `e ≥ 0` the dimension `σ_e := dim Syz_e/(trivial)` is a fixed integer, and for every `q > e`:
`dim (m^{[q]}R)_{q+e} = 5·HF(R)_e − σ_e`.
**The graded Hilbert function of the gap ideal throughout the window is independent of the tower level.** The tower has vanished from the census side of the wall.

**Corollary 2 (the solving tool).** Finding all window syzygies at coefficient degree e is the finite linear system: `λ_a − λ_b ∈ (s_a+s_b) + (ε₁^{ab}, ε₃^{ab})` over the 15 edges — 15 membership conditions in fixed ideals. In `R` the `ε₁`-slack collapses (`ε₁^{ab} ≡ −(s_a+s_b)` mod E): **the only genuinely new slack is `ε₃^{(ab)}`** — every syzygy beyond the even/known families must spend `ε₃`-slack on some edge.

**Anchors (byte-verified).** e = 0: σ = 0 (5 raw generators ✓ measured). e = 1: σ = 0 ✓. e = 2: σ = 1 = the `p_{q+2}` syzygy (trivial S₆-type) ✓ (74 = 75−1 at v=2). e = 3: σ = 5 = `p_{q+2}·R₁` (standard type) ✓ (165 = 170−5). e = 4: σ = 25, decomposing (evidence: dims + mod-3 trace) as `triv⊕triv⊕std⊕(4,2)⊕(4,2)` — known 16 + the extracted 9 (explicit machine specimens in hand; closed-form naming open).
